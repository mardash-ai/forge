# The Laws of Forge

> These are not guidelines.
>
> These are the laws that govern Forge.

The Laws are timeless. They describe what must remain true even as technology changes.

## 1. Software Is Built, Not Written

Writing code is an activity.

Building software is the craft.

Forge optimizes the craft.

## 2. If You Create Software, You Are a Builder

Humans build.

Agents build.

Services build.

Different forms.

One identity.

**Builder.**

## 3. Builders Pursue Goals

A Builder starts with a desired outcome.

The Goal is the source of work.

Do not confuse the Goal with the mechanism.

## 4. Capabilities Perform Behavior

A Capability is what Forge can do.

Plan.

Build.

Test.

Evaluate.

Deploy.

Observe.

Capabilities are behavior.

## 5. Resources Represent State

A Resource is what exists.

Builds.

Deployments.

Artifacts.

Evaluations.

Workspaces.

Resources are state.

## 6. Events Record Facts

Events describe what happened.

They do not ask for action.

They do not contain intent.

They are immutable facts.

## 7. Policies Govern

Policies decide what is allowed, required, blocked, or escalated.

Policies protect Builders, Resources, and the software being created.

## 8. Permissions Authorize

Permissions answer who may act.

Permissions are relationships between Builders and Capabilities or Resources.

## 9. Builders Own Outcomes

Agents increase leverage.

Automation increases speed.

Responsibility does not move.

Builders own outcomes.

## 10. Every Decision Helps Builders Build

Every feature.

Every Capability.

Every Resource.

Every Policy.

Every Permission.

Every architecture choice.

Every pull request.

Answers one question:

> Does this help Builders build?

If yes, continue.

If no, remove it.
